# Glossary of Physical AI Terms

## A

**Action**: A ROS 2 communication pattern for long-running tasks with feedback and goal management.

**Affordance**: The property of an object that indicates its possible use or function to an agent.

**Agent**: An autonomous system that perceives its environment and takes actions to achieve goals.

**Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)**: Hypothetical AI that demonstrates human-level intelligence across diverse domains.

## B

**Behavior Tree**: A hierarchical structure used for organizing and controlling robot behaviors.

**Bounding Box**: A rectangular box that encloses an object in 2D or 3D space, used for object detection.

## C

**Cartesian Space**: The 3D coordinate system used to describe positions and orientations in physical space.

**Cognitive Architecture**: A framework for organizing and implementing intelligent behaviors in robots.

**Command and Control**: The system responsible for interpreting commands and executing robot actions.

**Computer Vision**: The field of study focused on enabling computers to interpret and understand visual information.

**Control Theory**: The mathematical study of dynamical systems and their control using feedback.

## D

**Deep Learning**: A subset of machine learning using neural networks with multiple layers.

**Digital Twin**: A virtual replica of a physical system used for simulation and analysis.

**Dynamics**: The study of forces and torques that cause motion in mechanical systems.

## E

**Embodied AI**: Artificial intelligence that is integrated with physical systems to interact with the world.

**End-Effector**: The tool or device at the end of a robotic manipulator used to interact with objects.

**Environment**: The physical or simulated space in which a robot operates.

## F

**Forward Kinematics**: The process of calculating the position of a robot's end-effector from joint angles.

**Fiducial Marker**: A visual marker used for precise object localization and orientation.

## G

**Gazebo**: A physics-based 3D simulation environment for robotics.

**Generalization**: The ability of a system to perform correctly on new, unseen data or situations.

**Geometric Reasoning**: The ability to understand and manipulate spatial relationships.

## H

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)**: The study of interactions between humans and robots.

**Humanoid Robot**: A robot with human-like form and capabilities.

## I

**Inverse Kinematics**: The process of calculating joint angles needed to achieve a desired end-effector position.

**Isaac Sim**: NVIDIA's simulation environment for robotics and AI.

## J

**Joint Space**: The coordinate system defined by robot joint angles.

## K

**Kinematics**: The study of motion without considering the forces that cause it.

**Knowledge Representation**: The field of artificial intelligence focused on representing information about the world.

## L

**Language Model**: An AI model trained to understand and generate human language.

**Latent Space**: A compressed representation space used in machine learning models.

**LiDAR**: Light Detection and Ranging, a sensing technology using laser light.

## M

**Manipulation**: The ability of a robot to physically interact with objects in its environment.

**Manipulator**: A robotic device designed to manipulate objects.

**Mapping**: The process of creating a representation of the environment.

**Middleware**: Software that provides common services and capabilities to applications.

## N

**Navigation**: The ability of a robot to move through its environment to reach goals.

**Neural Network**: A machine learning model inspired by biological neural networks.

**Node**: A process in ROS that performs computation.

## O

**Obstacle Avoidance**: The capability to detect and navigate around obstacles.

**Occupancy Grid**: A 2D representation of environment space as occupied or free.

**OpenCV**: An open-source computer vision and machine learning software library.

## P

**Perception**: The ability to interpret sensory information to understand the environment.

**Physical AI**: The integration of artificial intelligence with physical systems and robotics.

**Point Cloud**: A set of data points in 3D space, typically from 3D sensors.

**Pose**: The position and orientation of an object in 3D space.

**Publisher**: A ROS node that sends messages on a topic.

## Q

**Quaternion**: A mathematical representation for 3D rotations and orientations.

## R

**Real-time**: Systems that respond to inputs within strict time constraints.

**Reinforcement Learning**: A type of machine learning where agents learn through interaction with an environment.

**Robot Operating System (ROS)**: Flexible framework for writing robot software.

**ROS 2**: The second generation of the Robot Operating System with improved architecture.

**Rigid Body**: An object that maintains its shape and size under forces.

**Robotics**: The field of technology focused on the design, construction, and operation of robots.

## S

**Sensor Fusion**: The process of combining data from multiple sensors to improve perception.

**Service**: A ROS communication pattern for request-response interactions.

**Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)**: The computational problem of constructing a map while localizing in it.

**Subscriber**: A ROS node that receives messages on a topic.

**System Integration**: The process of combining different subsystems into a unified system.

## T

**Task Planning**: The process of determining a sequence of actions to achieve goals.

**Teleoperation**: Remote operation of a robot by a human operator.

**Topic**: A ROS communication channel for passing messages between nodes.

**Trajectory**: A path with timing information for robot motion.

## U

**Unity**: A real-time 3D development platform used for simulation and visualization.

**Unstructured Environment**: An environment without predefined organization or constraints.

## V

**Vision-Language-Action (VLA)**: Systems that integrate visual perception, language understanding, and physical action.

**Visual Servoing**: Control of robot motion based on visual feedback.

## W

**Workspace**: The space in which a robot can operate.

## X, Y, Z

**XYZ**: The three-dimensional coordinate system axes (X, Y, Z).
